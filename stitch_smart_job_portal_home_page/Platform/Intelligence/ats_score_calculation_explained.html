Keywords Match

40% Impact

This is the single most critical factor. ATS scanners compare the words in your resume directly against the job description. Missing key skills often leads to an automatic rejection.

check_circle How to Optimize

  • Mirror Language: Use the exact phrasing found in the job post (e.g., "Project Management" vs "Managing Projects").
  • Hard Skills First: Prioritize tools, software, and technical methodologies over soft skills.
  • Context Matters: Don't just list keywords; weave them into your bullet points to show application.

Experience Relevance

30% Impact

The algorithm evaluates your job titles, years of experience, and the companies you've worked for. It looks for career progression and relevant industry exposure.

check_circle How to Optimize

  • Standard Titles: If your official title was obscure (e.g., "Guru"), use a standard equivalent in parentheses.
  • Quantify Results: Use numbers and percentages to demonstrate impact, which algorithms often interpret as high-quality content.

Formatting & Parsability

20% Impact

If the robot can't read it, it doesn't exist. Complex layouts, columns, graphics, and non-standard fonts can confuse the parser, resulting in a blank profile on the recruiter's end.

check_circle How to Optimize

  • Keep it Simple: Stick to single-column layouts for maximum compatibility.
  • Standard Headers: Use "Experience", "Education", and "Skills" as section headers. Don't get creative here.
  • File Type: Submit as .docx or a simple PDF generated from a text editor, not an image-based PDF.
school

Education & Certs

10% Impact

While weighted lower for experienced roles, proper listing of degrees and certifications is essential for passing hard filters (e.g., "Must have Bachelor's Degree").

check_circle How to Optimize

  • Spell it Out: Write out degree names fully (e.g., "Bachelor of Science") alongside abbreviations (B.S.).
